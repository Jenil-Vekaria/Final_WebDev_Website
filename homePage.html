DUBAI


About Dubai

Is there any place on Earth as extravagant as Dubai? Visitors can admire the city of Dubai from the 148th level of Burj Khalifa, the tallest building in the world. If you prefer to keep both feet on the ground, check out the 30 minutes show at the Dubai Fountain, the world’s second-largest water fountain choreographed with music and light which was designed by the same company behind Las Vegas’ dancing fountains at the Bellagio Hotel. Exquisite shopping can be found in Dubai along with out of this world activities such as indoor snow skiing, posh dining at Palm Jumeirah, and roller coaster rides at IMG Worlds of Adventure. For a traditional experience, see the Dubai Spice Market, go for a camel ride at Mushrif Park, or head over to Jumeirah Mosque for a tour.

ROME


About Rome

Rome is a classic destination filled with art, architecture, and food. Be sure to take in Michelangelo’s iconic painted ceiling at the Sistine Chapel, Apollo and Daphne, which is Bernini’s life-size intricate marble statue at the Galleria Borghese, and visit Galleria Borghese room VIII to see six Caravaggio paintings. Notable sites to visit in Rome include the Colosseum where gladiators battled, St. Peter’s Basilica, and Trevi Fountain. Be sure to grab a gelato on the go or enjoy a nice dinner of carbonara and wine while in Rome.

SHANGHAI


About Shanghai

Nothing’s quite as diverse as the city of Shanghai. Visitors can experience anything from the ancient and traditional to the modern and unique aspects of China. Be sure to see the Jade Buddha Temple, built in 1882, Qibao Ancient Town, which has a thousand-year-old history, and Yu Yuan Gardens, which was built over 400 years ago and features one of China’s famous jade sculptures. Modern marvels not to be missed include the viewing platform on the 88th floor of Jinmao Tower, the third tallest tower in the world, and the view of and from the futuristic Oriental Pearl TV Tower.
